MULTIPLAYER GAME

THAT AFFECT TRUST BETWEEN PLAYERS

This project seeks to verify that the best way to interpret and influence the balance of credibility (trust) between users is through graphical representations or conceptual aspects that influence the user psyche. We compared a terror interface that triggers the survival instinct of the players versus an interface that presents the same information in numerical numbers.

GAME'S PLOT

The story takes place in a house in the middle of nowhere. Our two characters (boy and girl) arrive at this place looking for a shelter on a rainy night, but the moment they enter the house, the traps are activated and both end in a different room. They must leave the house immediately because there is a monster that eats people living there, however, only one will leave alive.

USER FLOW

The objective of the game is simple: The first one that leaves the house wins.

In the game, you know the right way for your opponent to leave the house (win) but you do not know yours. The game is played by rounds and in each round the players must propose to their opponent a way to take. You decide whether to send to the other player the way to the exit or to the monster and your opponent will decide whether to believe you or not. The first one leaving the house win and the other will be eaten by the monster.

Reliability

After each round, it is shown to every user the percentage of reliability (0% - 25% - 50% - 100%) between the two players. This percentage depends on if an user tells the other to go through the rigth way or not and if the users decide to take the advice of the other user or not. Each user can see this percentage after each round.
